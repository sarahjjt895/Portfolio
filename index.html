RunWise | Run Tracking Application

Developed to provide runners a comprehensive platform for tracking, analyzing, and enhancing their performance. Integrating tracking metrics that allow the user to set their goals and provide insights on their daily, weekly and monthly accomplishments.

The Problem

Many running apps fail to fully meet the needs of avid runners, often lacking in customization, personalized feedback, and engaging features. Most current solutions provide basic tracking and goal-setting functionalities but fall short in delivering tailored insights or motivating content.


Runners are often left to settle for “good enough” rather than an app that truly enhances their training and performance.

The Solution

  • Dynamically tailored training plans that adapt based on individual performance data and progress.
  • Advanced Performance Insights that provide In-depth analysis and feedback on running form and efficiency.
  • Dynamic Goals that adjust based on user progress to keep motivation high.
  • Intuitive User Interface with seamless navigation and use across all devices.

UX Research Methodology

MARKET RESEACH

___________________

The Rise of Fitness Tracking Apps

In recent years, the popularity of fitness tracking apps has surged. As of 2023, there are over 300,000 health and fitness apps available on major platforms, compared to just 100,000 in 2018. This significant growth highlights a growing interest in health and wellness. Research shows that 80% of smartphone users have downloaded at least one health or fitness app, with 50% using them regularly.


Among these, running apps have seen a substantial increase, with users seeking to monitor their performance, set goals, and improve their overall fitness.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

___________________

On Track for Improvement


The leading running apps (Nike Run Club, Strava, Runkeeper, MapMyRun) offer a range of features, but many still fall short in terms of personalization and user engagement. Typical features include: .


  • GPS tracking of runs
  • Goal setting and achievement tracking
  • Social sharing and community features
  • Audio feedback and coaching
  • Integration with wearables and other fitness devices

However, these apps often lack advanced personalization options, such as tailored training plans based on individual performance data, or in-depth analysis and feedback on running form and efficiency. Users also report a desire for more engaging and motivating content, such as challenges and virtual races.

USER RESEARCH

___________________

Understanding Needs and Preferences

To gain deeper insights into the needs and preferences of runners, I conducted one-on-one interviews with 5 regular users of running apps. Additionally, I distributed a survey to 51 participants to validate these insights on a larger scale. Key findings from this research include:


  • Users value detailed performance tracking but want more personalized insights and recommendations based on their data.
  • While goal setting is popular, users seek more dynamic and adaptable goals that evolve with their progress.
  • There is a strong demand for customizable training plans and the ability to tailor the app to individual needs and preferences.

What would elevate your experience?

I identified three key enhancements during my one-on-one interviews, which were later validated through surveys where participants could also suggest additional features. This process revealed five preferred features for a runner's app, with detailed performance tracking emerging as the most favored, followed by improved goal setting and personalized plans tailored to individual user needs.

Problem Statement

As a seasoned runner, I want enhanced features for detailed performance tracking, improved goal setting, and tailored plans to better meet my individual running needs, thereby enabling me to achieve my fitness goals effectively and enjoy a more personalized running experience.

Wireframe Prototype


The initial prototype was designed with Balsamiq having the user flow in mind. The landing page allowed for a clear interface, only providing the most essential data.

  • User overlay screens include logging data for run session (distance, durations etc.), as well as providing mood insights after run completion.

The Problem

In an increasingly digital world, concerns are rising over the detrimental effects of technology on mental and physical well-being. The widespread use of digital communication platforms, social media, and smartphones has reduced face-to-face interactions, leading to heightened social anxiety across all age groups. This is a significant issue, impacting personal relationships, professional growth, and overall mental health.


The Solution

Addressing this problem led me to create "RealTalk." This holistic solution integrates:

  • Digital application
  • Physical toolkit
  • Community spaces

These componenets are designed to combat digital media-induced social anxiety while addressing related challenges like physical discomfort and declining attention spans. RealTalk has evolved through extensive research, user feedback, and multiple iterations.

User Research

I conducted comprehensive primary & secondary research to investigate the issue of social anxiety arising from digital media usage. Employing a multi-method approach, the study integrated an online ethnography, questionnaires, interviews, and individual personal reflections from within the research group.

Competitive Analysis

Upon reviewing various apps that target anxiety offered by competitors, it became clear that these apps were primarily designed to treat mental health overall, instead of focusing to treat people who suffer from social anxiety.

Given that a significant proportion of the questionnaire respondents fell within the 15-30 age bracket, I deliberately focused my interview efforts on individuals in this demographic. To synthesize this data, I conducted a thematic analysis in a search for common themes, patterns and insights.

Following the analysis of feedback from five expert usability tests conducted on the prototype, several critical issues have come to light. The application has been found to have shortcomings, such as screens lacking back buttons, and there is a need for an additional incentive system to motivate socially anxious individuals to engage in spontaneous challenges.

Design Brief

The brief for this design was to create the branding and mobile app prototype for a fitness company of our choosing, I selected long boarding (a form of skating on a board). We were tasked with using multiple technical programs. Specifically, Adobe XD for wireframing and prototyping, Illustrator for icon and logo generation and AfterEffects for animating. All assets and illustrations within this project were illustrated by myself.

The Problem

During initial ideation, I created wireframes and screens but realized they resembled countless other fitness apps. User research revealed a lack of options in existing apps that offered a holistic approach to covering all the needs of longboarders. This prompted me to rethink my approach and design an app that specifically addressed these unmet needs.

The Solution

Based on user research, the app required a friends page for connecting and meeting up with fellow skaters, a spot-finding tool to locate smooth roads and assess hill intensities, and an e-commerce section for purchasing gear. These tailored features set the app apart from generic fitness apps, ensuring it meets the distinct needs of the long-boarding community.

UX Research Methodology

Through research, we uncover the intricate tapestry of knowledge, woven with curiosity and discovery.

Competitive Analysis

The competitive analysis of similar apps reveals various strengths and weaknesses across different platforms. Each app has its unique features and user base, but several opportunities stand out for enhancing their offerings

  • Expand social features to build community.
  • Enhance spot-finding capabilities.
  • Add or improve e-commerce features.
  • Offer board repair services.
  • Increase profile and skateboard personalization options.
  • Provide more tutorials and learning resources.

Interviews

I conducted seven one-on-one interviews with avid skaters, which provided valuable insights into user needs and preferences. Participants highlighted the importance of social features, spot-finding capabilities, and access to tutorials.


These interviews have been instrumental in guiding the development of a more comprehensive and user-friendly long-boarding app.

Thematic Analysis

Thematic analysis of skater interviews guided the app design by highlighting key user preferences: strong social features, comprehensive spot-finding tools, quality learning resources, customization options, and integrated e-commerce and repair services.


These insights directed the focus towards creating a more engaging and functional app that meets the specific needs of skateboarders, enhancing community interaction and user experience.

Ideating & Sketching

On initial ideation, I began drawing out my wireframes and creating different screens but soon realized it looked similar to the hundreds, if not thousands, of other fitness apps out there. I scrapped that idea and tried to think of a problem I had recently encountered, where an app could provide the solution. This led me to the concept of an app that helps longboarders or cruisers find smooth roads that are easy to skate on, as well as identify the intensity levels of different hills on a map that can pinpoint their location.

Wireframe Prototype

I wanted the app to represent an easy-to-use interface, that had more than just a map for the users, so I added sections including a friends page (add friends, check their stats, find out if their cruising nearby for a meetup), shop for items like new boards, wheels etc., learn new tips and tricks, and check out inspiring skaters and cruisers.
